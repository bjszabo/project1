Background

My name is Barbara Szabo. I’m a 46 year old mother, wife, full time electrical designer, and part-time student. I am origninally from Chicago and have lived most of my life here. Though, I did live in Michigan for 10 years. My background is varied and life has taken me in unexpected directions. I have one child: my son, Raymond, is 21 years of age.

As a young person I had dreams of studying at MSOE (Milwaulkee School of Engineering) to become and Architect. However, due to circumstances beyond my control, I was unable to do so. Eventually I was able to pursue and AAS detree in CADD from ITT, while at the same time working two full-time jobs. In 1994, just before graduating, I started my career in the electrical world. First as an entry-level drafter, and learning and working my way up to the Senior electrical Designer postion that I hold today. reer (my son is 21 and moved out).

My design/engineering experience is varied. I have worked on projects for power plants, both nuclear and fossil fuel types, some renewable energy, substations, commercial and residential buildings, and constrols systems, to name a few. I have worked in many different programs such as AutoCad, Microstation, Visual lighting softare, among others. I thoroughly love what I do and constantly look for ways to learn and grow.

Current Status

I am currently enrolled as a part-time student at ITT in the process of pursuing my BSEE. I have been working towards this for many years and have had to fit school around life and family. Many ask me why do I still seek to get my BSEE since I’ve been in my career for 26 years now and am well-established. There are a few reasons for this. The first and simplest answer is because I want to. It’s alwasy good to do something for yourself that enriches you, and this is that for me. Second, because I enjoy it… the learning as well as the work. I want to grow in my knowledge and abilities. Not to mention learning continues to help the brain function. Third, even though I am capable of perfoming, and do perform engineering tasks, I need the degree in order to have more opportunities available to me. Hence, for me, it comes down to desire, enjoyment,and career necessity.

Additionally, I work full-time for a local engineering firm. I have been there for just over ten years now. My focus had firsst been mainly nuclear and coal powered power plants. For the last couple years or so I've been more involved in substation and stubstation security design. My currently role is as a physical design lead for one of our security design teams.
